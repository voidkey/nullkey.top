I'm nullkey — a software engineer broadcasting from the moon, somewhere in the solar system. I build small, sharp tools: sometimes for everyone, often just for myself.

Day to day I live in Go, Python and TypeScript, with detours into whatever lets me ship something useful before lunch.

> *balance is all you need.*

I've come to believe most things in life are not problems to solve but balances to hold. Work and rest. Speed and depth. Solitude and the people you love. Ambition and acceptance. Shipping and savoring. The world isn't a switch with two positions; almost everything good lives somewhere on a dial, and the craft is finding where to set it — and being willing to move it tomorrow.

The same is true for **AI**. Not *human or machine*, but how much of each, and when. I try to build *with* models rather than around them: let them go fast where speed is cheap, stay slow where judgment matters, and keep the dial in my own hand. Generation has gotten almost free; what stays scarce is taste, intent, and the willingness to ask better questions.

Balance isn't a compromise. It's the whole game — in code, in work, in love, in how you spend an afternoon. Get it right and everything else has room to breathe.

In a world that auto-completes, let thought remain your own.
